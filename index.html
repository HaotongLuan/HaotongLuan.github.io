Undergraduate researcher in robotics

I am Haotong Luan (栎皓童), an undergraduate researcher at Shenzhen Technology University and an X Scholar in the joint training program with Tsinghua University and Shenzhen X-Institute.

I work under the supervision of Prof. Wenzeng Zhang and am currently collaborating with Researcher Yuan Gao on visual-servoing control algorithms and end-effector design for robotic-arm grasping.

My work focuses on environment-adaptive robotic hands and mechanism design: linear-motion linkages, parallelogram mechanisms, underactuated and tendon-driven grippers, card-scooping hands, grasp-and-lift mechanisms, and idle-stroke transmissions.

research

01 · underactuated grippers

Linear-motion linkages, parallelogram mechanisms, and idle-stroke transmissions that embed grasping logic directly into the hardware, enabling reliable grasping with a single actuator.

dual-parallelogram · idle-stroke · single-actuator

02 · environment-adaptive hands

Hands that exploit contact with tables or walls to switch modes automatically, from pinching to scooping to lifting, robust to pose error and collision during manipulator operation.

card scooping · mode switching · contact-triggered

03 · mechanism design

Peaucellier and semi-Peaucellier linkages, slot-guided mechanisms, and hybrid parallelogram designs aimed at precise linear fingertip motion and posture-preserving grasping.

Peaucellier · slot-guided · linear linkage

04 · embodied intelligence (future)

Long-term interest in the intersection of mechanism design and embodied intelligence, coupling hardware-embedded logic with learning-based control for resource-constrained robotics.

embodied AI · hardware-software co-design
